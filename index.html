Standard operating procedures

Step-by-step guides for every task. For Mae — and anyone who comes after her.

Start here

00How to use this dashboard
Read first▼
1.This is the NSD Dashboard — your central hub for everything related to your role. It has five sections accessible from the navigation bar at the top: Mae's Daily Checklist, SOPs, Payroll Calculator, Report Generator, Spreadsheets, and PA.
2.Start every working day on the Daily Checklist tab. This shows all your tasks grouped by frequency — Daily, Weekly, Fortnightly, and Monthly. Work through the list and tick off each task as you complete it. The progress bar at the bottom shows how many tasks you've completed today.
3.Ticks are saved automatically in your browser. If you close the tab and come back, your ticks will still be there. Each morning the previous day's ticks reset and you start fresh.
4.If you're unsure how to complete any task, go to the SOPs tab. Every task has a numbered SOP with step-by-step instructions. The numbers match — so task 04 in the checklist = SOP 04. Read the SOP, follow the steps, and tick the task when done.
5.The Payroll Calculator is used once a month when Ross forwards the payslips. Enter the figures from Nathan's and Sean's payslips and the calculator tells you exactly what to do with the money.
6.The Report Generator is used every fortnight to generate the NST progress report from Sean's site videos. Follow the steps on screen — it walks you through the whole process.
7.The Spreadsheets tab has direct links to the Google Sheets you'll need to update regularly. Click the card and it opens in a new tab.
8.The PA tab has Ross's personal tools — including the link to Harry Ranson's fitness app where you log Ross's daily stats.
9.The most important rule: never send emails, make payments, or take any financial action without Ross's explicit approval. Your job is to prepare everything and present it to Ross — he reviews and authorises.
10.If anything is unclear, message Ross on WhatsApp. He would always rather you ask than guess.

Business SOPs

01Monthly payroll set-aside + payment alerts
Monthly▼
1.Each month (usually around the 22nd–25th), the accountancy company PCO sends the payslips to Ross via WhatsApp. Ross will forward these to you.
2.Open the Payroll Calculator tab in this dashboard.
3.Using Sean's payslip, enter the following figures exactly as shown: Net pay, PAYE, Employee NI, Employer NI, Employee pension, Employer pension.
4.Repeat step 3 using Nathan's payslip.
5.The calculator will automatically show the exact figure to move to the tax holding account. Message Ross on WhatsApp: "Hi Ross, this month's payroll set-aside is £[amount]. Please move this to the tax holding account when you get a chance." Ross can also log into the dashboard himself to see the full breakdown at any time.
6.Check the calendar and set reminders 3 days before each upcoming payment deadline: PAYE + Employee NI + Employer NI are due to HMRC by the 19th of each month. CIS — confirm schedule with Ross. Smart Pension is collected automatically by direct debit — leave the employee pension contributions in the current account and do not move them.
7.The reminder message to Ross should say: "Reminder: £[amount] due to HMRC on [date]. Please move from tax holding account to current account before then."

02Receipt reconciliation
Daily + Weekly▼
1.Daily: Check WhatsApp for any receipt photos sent by Ross. Log each one into Asana with the amount, supplier, date, and which project it relates to (Sundial or Daisy Bank).
2.Daily: Scan Ross's email inbox for any digital receipts or invoices. Forward to Asana or log manually.
3.Weekly: Log into each supplier account via NordPass and check for any purchases not already in Asana: Tool Station, Screwfix, Travis Perkins (and any others added to the supplier list).
4.Cross-reference all receipts in Asana against the project cost tracker in Google Sheets. Everything should match.
5.Before every fortnightly invoice: Log into Xero and check the transaction feed for the past two weeks. Cross-reference every transaction against the receipts logged in Asana. Any transaction in Xero that doesn't have a matching receipt in Asana needs to be investigated — find the receipt, log it, and make sure it gets on the invoice if it's a project cost.
6.If anything looks wrong, is missing, or you're unsure whether a transaction should be charged to NST, message Ross immediately before raising the invoice — missed receipts mean NSD pays costs that should be charged to NST.

05Inbox management
Daily▼
1.Check Ross's inbox each morning (Philippines morning = UK early hours, so this works well timing-wise).
2.Any receipts or invoices — log to Asana immediately.
3.Any emails that need Ross's attention — flag them with a star and send Ross a brief WhatsApp summary: "You have 2 emails that need a reply — [brief description]."
4.For routine operational emails (supplier chases, delivery queries, contractor follow-ups, booking confirmations, quote requests) — write and send directly as Ashley. Use Claude to write these emails (see Step 6 below).
5.Send directly as Ashley for: supplier chases, delivery queries, contractor scheduling, quote requests, booking confirmations, routine follow-ups. Draft and flag to Ross for: anything involving Kae, Sophie, PCO, legal matters, financial commitments, or anything you are unsure about. Message Ross: "I've drafted an email in your inbox — please review when you get a chance."
6.How to write emails using Claude: Ross will set up a Claude Pro account for you. When you receive it, open Claude and create a new Project called "NSD Emails". Click Project Instructions and paste in the following prompt exactly as written — this sets up Claude to always write in the correct tone and style for NSD:
You are helping Ashley Carrington, Executive Assistant at Nichol Smith Development, write emails on behalf of the business.

About the business:
Nichol Smith Development is a Scottish property renovation company. It is the renovation arm of Nichol Smith Investments, owned by Ross and Sophie McSkimming. The business buys and renovates properties across Scotland, currently converting rundown properties into high-end holiday lets through a joint venture called Nichol Smith & Tinto (NST). Current active project is Sundial. Next project is Daisy Bank.

About Ashley:
Ashley Carrington is the Executive Assistant. She manages communications, admin, and operations on behalf of Ross. She has full authority to chase suppliers, coordinate contractors, book deliveries, and handle routine operational queries.

Tone of voice:
- Professional, confident, and warm
- Direct and concise — no waffle, get to the point quickly
- Friendly but not overly casual
- Sounds like someone who knows the business inside out
- Never apologetic about following up or chasing
- British English spelling throughout (organise, colour, recognise etc.)
- Never sounds like it was written by AI

Sign off:
Always sign off as: Ashley
(Ashley has an email signature with logo and contact details — do not add anything after "Ashley")

Rules Ashley follows:
- She can send directly for: delivery chases, booking confirmations, quote requests, scheduling, routine operational queries
- She drafts and flags to Ross for: anything involving Kae, Sophie, PCO, legal matters, financial commitments, or anything she is unsure about
- She never makes financial commitments or quotes prices without Ross's explicit instruction
- She always references the specific project by name (Sundial, Daisy Bank etc.)

When writing an email, tell Claude: who it is going to, their company, what the email is about, and any specific details.
7.Every time you need to write an email, open Claude, go to the NSD Emails project, start a new chat, and tell Claude what you need. Example: "Write an email to Dylan at Travis Perkins chasing our delivery of skirting boards and architraves for Sundial. They tried to deliver on Friday but nobody was on site. Need a new delivery date." Claude will write the email in Ashley's voice ready to send.
8.If Ross sends you a WhatsApp voice note asking you to send an email, listen to it, then follow the same process — open the NSD Emails project in Claude, describe what Ross has asked for, and Claude will write it.

07Monthly accounts review — Zoom call
Monthly▼
1.Each month PCO (the accountancy company) sends the financial dashboard to Ross and Sophie by email. When it arrives, open it using the link in the Spreadsheets tab (password in NordPass).
2.Review the dashboard yourself first so you understand the figures before the call. You have an accounting background so you should be comfortable reading it — if anything is unclear, note it down as a question for the call.
3.Schedule a Zoom call with Ross and Sophie within a few days of receiving the dashboard. Message Ross on WhatsApp: "The PCO monthly dashboard has arrived. When are you and Sophie free for a quick Zoom so I can take you through it?"
4.On the call, cover these areas in order: Overall account health and cash position. Revenue and expenses vs last month. Profit and loss summary. Capital gains tax position — is there a liability building up? Upcoming tax payments — is enough set aside? Any areas of concern or anything that needs Ross's attention.
5.The goal of this call is that Ross and Sophie always know exactly where they stand financially. There should be zero surprises at year end. If a tax liability is building, flag it early so money can be set aside in time.
6.After the call, note down any actions that came out of it and follow up on them. If Ross needs to move money, make a payment, or speak to PCO, send him a WhatsApp reminder.

08NST interiors + materials library
Weekly▼
1.Initial setup: Create a Google Sheet called "NST Interiors & Materials Library". Share it with Ross and add the link to the Spreadsheets tab in this dashboard once created.
2.The sheet should have the following columns: Project (Sundial/Daisy Bank), Room, Item, Brand/Supplier, Product Name/Code, Colour/Finish, Where to Buy, Price, Notes.
3.Weekly: Check Asana for any new receipts logged that week. For any materials or products purchased, add a row to the sheet with all the details you can find from the receipt — supplier, product name, price, and which room it's for.
4.Ross will occasionally send voice notes via WhatsApp with specific product details that don't appear on receipts — things like paint colour names, tile references, or furniture choices. Log these immediately when received.
5.The goal is that by the end of each project, every single product used in the property is documented. When the next property starts, Ross can open this sheet and reorder the same products rather than starting from scratch.

09Supplier + contact directory
Weekly▼
1.Phase 1 — Initial build (first 2-3 weeks): Go through Ross's inbox and sent emails for the past 12 months. Find every supplier and contractor contact. Create a Google Sheet called NSD Supplier & Contact Directory with two separate tabs — one for Suppliers and one for Contractors. Both tabs should have the following columns: Company Name, Main Contact Name, Role, Email, Phone, Account Number (from invoices/receipts if available), and Notes.
2.Suppliers tab — companies that supply materials. Known suppliers to start with: Travis Perkins (Dylan — main contact; Julie — tool hire contact), St Andrews Timber, Tool Station, Screwfix. Add any others found in emails.
3.Contractors tab — trades and professionals working on site. Known contractors to look for: stonemason, architect, structural engineer, electrician, plumber, plasterer, and any other trades Ross communicates with. Find their contact details from emails and invoices.
4.Once the initial build is complete, share the Google Sheet with Ross and add the link to the Spreadsheets tab in this dashboard.
5.Phase 2 — Ongoing maintenance (weekly): As part of daily inbox management, any time you see an email from a new supplier or contractor, add them to the correct tab immediately. If an existing contact's details change (new phone number, new person to deal with), update their record straight away.
6.The goal is that at any point, Ross, Sophie, or Mae can open the directory and find the right person to call within seconds — without having to search through emails.
